Prepare for Your First Class Session (Adobe Connect)

Please follow these best practice guidelines to ensure that you can fully participate in each Class Session:

  1. Use a hard-wired Internet connection. A hard-wired internet connection (Ethernet) is highly recommended. Weak Wi-Fi will cause issues such as latency, choppy audio, and loss of connection.
  2. Use Firefox. Google Chrome currently causes issues with webinar platforms, so we recommend Firefox or another browser for best results.
  3. Purchase a microphone/headset. Prepare to talk a lot during our Class Sessions! While many laptops come with built-in mics and speakers, we highly recommend that you use a USB-based headset with microphone, such as the Logitech ClearChat Comfort USB Headset. If you are using a laptop with a built-in microphone, please use headphones or earbuds during the Class Session to avoid echo or feedback. Listening through speakers may cause an echo that disrupts the entire class.
  4. Conduct a Meeting Diagnostic. Conduct a meeting connection diagnostic to make sure that your computer is ready for Adobe Connect meetings. You need a modern web browser, a supported version of Flash, and an Adobe Connect add-in.
  5. Test your mic and sound in our Adobe Connect Test Room. You can do so by logging in as a guest to our Adobe Connect Test Room, and following the instructions posted. We have made a quick-start guide to Adobe Connect for your reference.
  6. Submit a support ticket if you need help.
