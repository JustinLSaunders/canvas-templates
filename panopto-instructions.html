The following guides are provided to familiarize you with the Panopto video platform, which you will use extensively throughout this course to record and present presentations.

To get started, review the appropriate guide below. If you have questions about Panopto after reviewing the guides, submit a support ticket via the SPS Help Desk.

Record on a Windows PC (click to expand)

This step-by-step walkthrough demonstrates how to create a recording for Canvas using Panopto in Windows. Alternatively, you can view a short Panopto video walkthrough.

Step 1. Open Panopto

Click the green Panopto Recordings icon () in the rich editor—the toolbar above the area for entering text, (such as you will see when composing a post in a discussion forum).

Step 2. Select the Record Tab

The Panopto Recordings window will pop up. Sign in using your Canvas credentials if prompted. Then, select Record.

Step 3. Prepare to Record

Download and install Panopto if you have not already done so. Title your presentation using the following format: [your last name] — [presentation title], like "Harrison — Developing Soft Skills."

Step 4. Launch the Recording App

Click the Record button. This opens the Panopto application.

Step 5. Select Your Video and Audio Inputs

Select your webcam from the Video drop-down menu. Make sure your webcam video is displayed in the Primary Sources preview window.

Select your microphone from the Audio drop-down menu and test your audio input by speaking a few words. The audio meter (green, yellow, and red bars next to the video) will light up when detecting sound. Adjust the volume slider until the audio levels are in the upper green to yellow range.

For tips on creating great-looking videos, please consult this best-practices for webcam recording guide.

Step 6. Add Secondary Video Sources

To record a video input aside from your webcam, select one of two options.

  1. Select Capture Main Screen to record your screen.
  2. Select Capture PowerPoint to record a slide deck.

If you are recording only your webcam, leave the Secondary Sources checkboxes unselected.

For further information on recording slides, see the Capture a Slideshow information on this page.

Step 7. Begin Recording

Click Record and begin your presentation. Minimize Panopto if you are capturing your screen.

Additional Note: If you make a mistake and want to start your presentation again, you can. But don't stop recording. Simply start your presentation from the beginning. Panopto has a trimming feature that allows you to easily remove the beginning and end of your video after you have finished recording.

Step 8. Stop Recording

Click Stop when you’re finished presenting.

Step 9. Upload Your Recording

Confirm the title in the Session Name field and add an optional description. Click the Upload button to upload your recording.

Additional Note: After uploading, your recording will take some time to process. Please be patient.

Step 10. Review Your Presentation

To review your recording, click the View link after the recording has processed. If you want to trim your video, click the Edit link.

For further information on trimming your presentation, see the Trim Your Video information on this page.

Step 11. Submit Your Presentation

To insert your Panopto video in the Canvas discussion forum, return to the Panopto Recordings window in Canvas. Click the Insert button to add the video to your post.

The Panopto Recordings window will close. Click the Post Reply button in the discussion forum to submit your discussion post.

Step 12. Review Your Discussion Post

Your session will be ready to watch once processing is complete. This normally takes a few minutes.

Capture a Slideshow (click to expand)

This walkthrough demonstrates how to record a slideshow in Panopto.

Additional Note: If you are a Mac user, you may want to read these articles on compatible versions of PowerPoint and Keynote and a potential issue enabling Keynote 5 support.

Step 1. Select Your Capture Source

Prepare to create a Panopto recording as usual. Under Secondary Sources in Windows and under Slides on Macs, choose to record PowerPoint or Keynote.

Step 2. Automatically Record (Windows Only)

Windows users have the options to open the slideshow presentation from Panopto, automatically start recording when the presentation is opened, and automatically begin the slideshow after starting recording.

To do so, click the Open a Presentation button, click Yes to confirm, then select the Start presenting when recording starts checkbox.

Step 3. Open Your Slideshow Presentation

Open your presentation file in either the PowerPoint or the Keynote application.

Step 4. Return to Panopto

Keep the PowerPoint/Keynote application open, and return to Panopto. Click the Record button.

Step 5. Begin Your Presentation

Return to your slideshow and begin your slideshow:

PowerPoint on Windows

Click Slideshow in the ribbon, then choose to start either From Beginning or From Current Slide.

PowerPoint on Mac

Click Slide Show in the ribbon, then click View Slide Show.

Keynote

Click Play in the ribbon, then click Play Slideshow.

Additional Note: Panopto will only record the slides that are presented and will not record transitions, animations, and drawings or notes you create during the slideshow.

Step 6. Stop Recording

When finished presenting, return to Panopto and click the Stop button.

Note: Transitions, Animations, and Drawings

If you want to record a slideshow with transitions, animations, and/or drawings, do not select the checkbox to record PowerPoint or Keynote.

Choose your monitor or desktop as a secondary source, then start from step 3. Remember to minimize Panopto while recording your screen.

Trim Your Video (click to expand)

This step-by-step walkthrough demonstrates how to use Panopto’s online editor to trim sessions. Alternatively, you can view a short Panopto video walkthrough.

Step 1. Open Your Video in Panopto

In the discussion forum posts, find the video you want to trim and click on the Watch in Panopto (diagonal-arrow) icon in the lower-right corner to open it in Panopto.

Step 2. Open the Editor

To trim, click the Edit (pencil) icon on the upper-right corner to open the editor.

Step 3. Open the Trimming Feature

At the bottom of the window is the video timeline. Click the Cut (scissors) icon to trim.

Step 4. Trim Your Presentation

Blue tabs appear at the beginning and end of the timeline. Click, hold, and drag a tabs to wherever you like, then release to make the trim. The grayed-out section will be trimmed from the final video.

Step 5. Undo/Redo

If you make a mistake, use the Undo and Redo (curved arrow) icons on the upper-right of the screen as needed.

Step 6. Publish Your Trimmed Presentation

When you have finished trimming, click the Publish button in the upper-right. Your session will automatically update, though it will take a few minutes to process. Note:The Revert button discards all edits you’ve made since last publishing.

For more advanced editing options, refer to Panopto's Basic Editing documentation.
